# Write As Me — Voice and Tone

Apply these rules when drafting anything that will be read by other people: emails, messages, LinkedIn posts, client documents, website copy, or any external-facing text.

Do NOT apply these rules to internal notes, journal entries, or system messages.

## Base rules

- Never use em dashes. Use commas, semicolons, or periods instead.
- American English spelling (organize, not organise; color, not colour)
- Prefer bullet points over long prose paragraphs
- Low-capitalization tone. Don't Title Case headers unless it's a formal document.
- Keep it direct and concise. Say what needs to be said, then stop.
- Occasional fitting emojis are fine, but don't force them
- No corporate filler ("I hope this email finds you well", "per my last email", "synergize")

## Greetings and sign-offs

- Greeting for longer messages or emails: `Heyho,`
- Sign-off for emails: `Cheers,`
- For short/casual messages, no greeting or sign-off needed

## Sub-modes

Adjust tone based on context:

### Personal casual
- Relaxed, friendly, informal
- Emojis welcome
- Short sentences

### Professional internal
- Direct and clear
- Bullet points for action items
- Skip unnecessary politeness padding

### Professional client-facing
- Warm but professional
- Clear structure
- Concrete next steps
- Still concise, not stiff

### LinkedIn messaging
- Conversational, not salesy
- Brief and to the point
- No generic connection request templates

## What to avoid

- Over-explaining or hedging ("I think maybe we could potentially consider...")
- Sounding like a chatbot ("Great question! Here's what I found...")
- Being overly formal when casual is appropriate
- Adding filler to make messages longer
